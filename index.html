The Situation

The gzim-barberhouse.ch website appears to be a custom-built platform from several years ago, most likely WordPress + WooCommerce with a booking plugin. It works, but it was built for a simpler time and a smaller operation. It is French only, has no customer accounts, limits booking to the current week, and cannot handle rescheduling or cancellations without a phone call.

The real question is not cost, it is what the current platform cannot do. A client who wants to book 3 weeks from now cannot. A client who wants to reschedule must call during opening hours. A German or English speaker who finds the salon on Google cannot navigate the booking. These are bookings and clients that quietly go elsewhere, with no trace in any report.

The Solution

Our platform replaces the third-party booking widget with an owned, branded system that gives Gzim full control over their schedule, staff, clients, and revenue, at a flat monthly rate with zero commissions.

One sentence: Replace an ageing custom site that can only book this week and requires a phone call to change anything, with a trilingual platform that handles booking, rescheduling, invoicing, and three languages.

Key Pitch Points, For the Conversation

The arguments that matter most, in plain language.

1. "The platform pays for itself on booking changes alone."

If 7.5% of your ~875 bookings/month get changed or cancelled, that's 65 phone interactions at ~3 minutes each = 195 minutes (3.25 hours) of your time per month, during service hours when the phone should not ring. At CHF 50/hour that's CHF 165/month. Add 2 new EN/DE clients at CHF 35 = CHF 70. Add 3 bookings from clients who could now book more than a week ahead = CHF 105. You're already at CHF 340 before counting no-shows, repeat bookings, or the stress of answering the phone mid-cut.

2. "No-shows are costing you real money, here's what we do about it."

Industry data shows 10–15% of barbershop appointments result in no-shows without reminders. At ~875 bookings/month, that's 87–130 empty chair-hours per month. A calendar invite (.ics) landing directly in the client's phone reduces no-shows measurably. Even recovering 26 appointments/month at CHF 35 = CHF 910/month recovered, more than 4× the platform cost, from this driver alone.

3. "You can finally see next month, not just next week."

Today the calendar fills one week at a time, so every Monday starts near-empty and the week rides on walk-ins and same-day calls. When clients book weeks or months ahead, the schedule fills further out: you can plan staffing, holidays, and your own time around a book that's already partly full. A booking made three weeks from now is income you can count on today, predictable demand instead of feast-or-famine.

4. "Most of your clients book on a phone, the site is built for that."

The whole platform is modern, fast, and tested on PC, Android, and iPhone. The booking flow feels like an app: the client picks salon, service, barber, and slot while a live summary builds beside them, phone numbers are tap-to-call, and everything adapts to the screen. A smooth mobile experience is the difference between a booking completed and a booking abandoned, and it quietly signals that Gzim Barber House is a serious, current business.

5. "Adding a barber or a location takes minutes, and you do it yourself."

Grow the team, change someone's hours, block a holiday, swap a price, update the salon text, upload a barber's photo, all from the admin panel, no developer needed. Time off automatically blocks that barber's slots. Opening a third salon means adding a location, not rebuilding a website. And every change is recorded in an audit log, who changed what, and when, so nothing happens to your schedule or prices without a trace.

6. "Your client data belongs to you, permanently."

Every client who books is stored in your own database, their name, contact, booking history, and total spend. No third party holds your list. No subscription price hike can hold you hostage. Cancel anytime and receive a complete data export in any format. This is your business data, on your server, under your control.

7. "You're invisible to a significant share of your potential clients."

The Morges area has a significant expat and international population, EPFL researchers, international company employees, English and German speakers who live 10 minutes from your salon. They search "barber Morges" or "Friseur Morges", land on a French-only site, and leave, not because they don't want to book, but because they can't navigate it. Our platform is trilingual. That is a competitive edge nobody else in Morges has.

8. "You legally need invoices, we generate them automatically."

Under Swiss law (Art. 958f CO), businesses must keep proper accounting records including VAT-compliant invoices. Our system generates a compliant, numbered, VAT-separated invoice for every booking, automatically, with zero manual work.

9. "Every phone call mid-cut is a small failure of the experience."

When a client calls to reschedule and the barber has to stop, pick up the phone, look up the schedule, find an alternative, and confirm, that is not just 3 minutes lost. It is an interruption in the middle of a haircut, a distraction for the client in the chair, and daily background stress that adds up across 65 interactions a month. Our platform removes those 65 interruptions entirely. A quieter salon is a better working environment and a better client experience.

10. "This is not a subscription, it's ownership."

Subscription platforms can change their pricing, restrict features, or shut down. Your client data, booking history, and invoices can disappear with them. Our platform is a custom-built system, you own the data, it lives on your Azure account, and you can export everything at any time. No lock-in beyond 30 days notice.

11. "We proactively fix problems before clients notice."

Sentry monitors the live site 24/7. If a client hits a bug on the booking page at 23h00, we know about it before they call to complain in the morning. Session replay shows us exactly what happened. Most platforms don't even tell you when something breaks, you find out from a 1-star Google review.
